The Pacific Ring of Fire

The Pacific Ring of Fire is a horseshoe-shaped zone surrounding the Pacific Ocean where most earthquakes and volcanoes in the world occur.

  • 40,000 km: Length of the zone.
  • 90%: Of world's earthquakes.
  • 75%: Of world's active volcanoes.

Causes: Tectonic Movement

  • Subduction: One plate sinks under another.
  • Collision: Plates crash together.
  • Sliding: Plates slide past each other.
